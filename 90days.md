# FirstDATA — 90-Day Action Plan

> Modeled after Alex Hormozi's framework from *$100M Offers*.
> Budget: under $1,000 upfront. Goal: first paying customer as fast as possible.

---

## The One Person to Learn From: Alex Hormozi

He built a service business from zero with no brand, no audience, and no money — using outreach, an irresistible offer, and relentless follow-through. His *$100M Offers* framework is the exact playbook for packaging what you do into something people feel stupid saying no to. Everything else he teaches is free on YouTube.

---

## The Core Mindset Shift

Stop selling "AI Automation and Data Engineering." Nobody wakes up wanting that. They wake up wanting to stop losing 3 hours a day on manual reports. Sell the outcome, not the service.

Your job for 90 days is one thing: **get one client to pay you money**. Not build the perfect website. Not perfect your pitch deck. One paying client.

---

## Month 1 — Days 1–30: Build the Offer, Start the Outreach

### Week 1 — The Grand Slam Offer (3 days of work)

Create one specific entry-point offer. Not "we do AI Automation." This:

> **"The Data & Automation Audit"**
> We spend 2 weeks inside your operations, identify every manual task costing your team 5+ hours/week, and hand you a step-by-step automation roadmap with ROI estimates — for $997. If we don't find at least $5,000/year in recoverable time, you pay nothing.

That's your door. It's low risk for them, and it almost always converts to a full engagement because you've already proven you understand their business.

### Weeks 2–4 — LinkedIn Outreach (20 DMs/day, every day)

**Target:** COOs, Ops Directors, IT Managers, Finance Directors at companies with 20–200 employees.
**Filter by:** manufacturing, logistics, healthcare admin, finance — any ops-heavy industry.
**Bonus filter:** Azure or SQL Server visible in their company's job postings.

**The message — do NOT pitch:**

> "Hey [Name] — I noticed [Company] is running [Azure/SQL Server]. I work with ops teams to find and eliminate their biggest manual data bottlenecks. Quick question — is reporting or data cleanup still eating hours of your team's week?"

That's it. Wait for the reply. Have a conversation. Then offer the audit.

## Month 2 — Days 31–60: Close, Deliver, Document

**Get your first paid audit by Day 35.** With 20 DMs/day that's 280 touch points in 2 weeks. Even at a 1% response rate, that's 2–3 conversations. One of them closes.

### Deliver Like Your Reputation Depends on It — Because It Does

Document everything during the audit:
- Every finding
- Every screenshot
- Every hour-saved calculation

This becomes your case study, your testimonial, and your next proposal.

### The Referral Ask (around Day 45)

The moment the client says "this is great" — and they will — you say:

> "I'm glad this is landing. I only work with 3–4 clients at a time to keep quality high. Do you know one or two other ops leaders who are dealing with the same kind of mess? I'd be happy to give them the same audit."

One happy client in a connected industry = 2–3 warm introductions. Warm referrals close 10× faster than cold outreach.

## Month 3 — Days 61–90: Convert to Retainer, Stack Clients

### The Retainer Conversation (around Day 65)

After delivering the audit, you have the easiest pitch in the world:

> "We found $X in recoverable time. Now we build it. I have a 90-day implementation engagement at $8,000, and after that most clients move to a $2,500/month retainer so we can keep optimizing and adding automations as the business grows. Want to move forward?"

You're not selling. You're describing the logical next step they already want.

### Keep the Outreach Running

Don't stop the 20 DMs/day even while delivering. Pipeline dries up the moment you stop. While you're building for Client 1, you're closing Client 2.

## The Single Rule That Determines If This Works

> **20 personalized outreach messages every weekday. No exceptions. Not 19. Not "I'll catch up tomorrow." 20.**

Everything else is noise. The audit offer, the website, the case studies — none of it matters if nobody sees it. Volume of outreach is the only variable you fully control in Month 1. Do the reps.
